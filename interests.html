INTERESTS

Honestly, my whole life my favorite hobbly of all time is reading books.1 Therefore, my interests revolve around books, and I find great joy in exploring different genres.2 Reading every book out there is like soothing my soul, and I can guarantee that it brings me the utmost happiness.3 Although I’m more into reading fiction books, my choices often depend on what interests me or the mood I’m in on a particular day.4 I usually read romance genres, but I’ve always challenged myself to explore books outside my comfort zone.5 I read fantasy genres to expand my imagination, and I dive into classics to strengthen and deepen my vocabulary.6 However, what I don’t particularly enjoy about reading fantasy is that each book often introduces a new world, and understanding its structure is necessary to grasp the plot.7 Genres that I admittedly find intriguing are crime and thrillers, as I love scenes so intense that I can’t put the book down, sometimes forgetting to eat or do other tasks.8 What also interests me is going outside the house alone, without listening to music, as it feels intimate and allows me to reflect on life.9 I am also interested in playing different games, and when I become obsessed with one, I can easily spend an entire day on it without realizing I haven’t accomplished anything else.10 Coincidentally, I enjoy watching anime and reading manga or manhwa, which are illustrated stories that bring narratives to life in a different way.11 I think reading these types of books is a great way to visualize specific events, making the storytelling even more immersive.12 Ironically, I also love reading fantasy-based manga and manhwa because, unlike novels where you rely on imagination, these stories already have vivid illustrations.13 Beyond reading, I occasionally indulge in writing short stories or journaling my thoughts, as it helps me process emotions and ideas.14 I also enjoy discussing books with friends or fellow readers, as sharing perspectives often leads to deeper insights about the stories.15 When I read a book with a compelling plot twist, I find myself rereading certain pages just to analyze how the author built up to that moment.16 Sometimes, I even annotate my books, underlining thought-provoking quotes or making notes in the margins.17 Despite my love for physical books, I also appreciate e-books, as they allow me to carry an entire library wherever I go.18 I have an ever-growing list of books I want to read, and no matter how many I finish, the list only seems to get longer.19 At the end of the day, reading isn’t just a hobby for me—it’s a passion that shapes the way I see the world and fuels my curiosity for more stories.20
